Matchbox Replica Reflection

Here I will discuss my matchbox replica project, which introduced me to 3D Printing!

Guidlines

The guidelines for this project were straightforward. We were expected to create a 3D model and print a 3D matchbox car to scale. We tried to keep the X, Y, and Z measures at a 1:1 Scale of our model matchbox car. I used websites such as Onshape and Cura, to help create and print my model car. 

Process

The process for this project was very important, every step needed to be precise and correct. My first focus was the Drawing. I chose a small matchbox sports car. I used a vernier caliper to exactly measure all 3 dimensions of the car. I then took pictures of my engineering drawing and uploaded them to Onshape. I had to 3D model and use my dimension engineering drawing to coordinate the size and shape of the car. I then used the extrusion tool to remove negative space to give me the raw exterior of the car. I then added small details such as wheels and a spoiler. I then exported it as an STL file and brought the file into Cura, so I could finally print my car! I had to add supports to my car to ensure for a successful print. I then exported it as a GCODE file and chose a dark blue color for its color.

Setbacks and Solutions

This was one of the most fun and challenging projects yet. I faced — setbacks and solutions throughout the design process. I struggled with transferring my dimension sketch into Onshape, and fitting the right dimensions on the rectangle. I ended up having to remeasure all three dimensions again to have them fit on the box. I also struggled with creating wheels on my car. I decided to go with a different wheel style than everyone else. I made my car a low rider, and decided to show only half of the wheels, creating a retro look.  I think that was the highlight of my design and a great solution to one of my obstacles.
